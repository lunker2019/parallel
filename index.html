Welcome to the Parallel Maths Project

Parallel is a weekly bulletin written by Dr Simon Singh, author of the No. 1 bestseller Fermat’s Last Theorem.

Every week, participating students will receive an email that contains 60 minutes of interesting, fun and challenging maths that goes beyond school maths: mystery and history, activities and oddities, puzzles and problems.

Parallel is designed for students in Year 7 and Year 8 (age 11 upwards). The pilot will run for four weeks, starting on June 30, and more will come in September.

Parallelograms

Noun: parallelogram

Pronounced: /ˌparəˈlɛləɡram/

Origin: late 16th century: from French parallélogramme, via late Latin from Greek parallēlogrammon, from parallēlos ‘alongside another’ + grammē ‘line’.

1. a four-sided plane rectilinear figure with opposite sides parallel.

2. a portmanteaux word combining parallel and telegram. A message containing mischievous maths problems sent each week by the Parallel Project to bright young mathematicians.
